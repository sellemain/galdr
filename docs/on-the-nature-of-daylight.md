The hold is immediate. Not built toward — the first notes arrive and you're already inside, the room reorganized before you've decided to listen. This is not a piece that earns your attention through variation or surprise. It takes you on contact, something in the opening harmonic landing differently in the body than you expected, a small weight settling.

At 0:06, a silence less than a second long — -70.8dB, pressure rather than absence. And then the pattern re-locks and holds for the next six and a half minutes without interruption.

What holds you is not movement. Melodically, 72% of the arc is sustained pitch — the remaining fraction tilting slightly south of flat, trending nowhere at a pace too slow to feel like direction. The breath is in near-perfect equilibrium: building and releasing in near-identical thirds, with sustaining fractionally dominant. Nothing is accumulating toward a release. Nothing has been withheld that the piece intends to return. The balance is too precise to be accident. This is not a piece that failed to build. It decided not to.

What holds you instead is harmonic mass. Pure tonal warmth — no noise, no percussive texture, nothing breaking the resonance field. And the major/minor balance sits at essentially zero: not major, not minor, suspended in the gap between them. The piece carries no tonal argument. It doesn't reach for joy or grief. It occupies something older and less specific — a condition of listening, rather than a feeling that listening produces. You cannot place it emotionally. Which means you stop trying. Which means your attention goes somewhere it doesn't usually go when it's busy categorizing.

The harmonic voices carry barely enough presence to register as presence. Enough to hold the weight. Not enough to declare melody. The tension stays low across the full arc. The tempo runs beneath the surface — metronomic, steady, not organizing your attention around the beat but providing a pulse the way a heartbeat provides one: you'd notice its absence.

Around two minutes something settles in the chest — not recognition, but the body registering that it will not need to brace. The piece is not building toward anything. There is nowhere to be late for.

The thing that happens after that is harder to describe. The piece doesn't change — the harmonic mass holds constant, the breath stays balanced, the melodic arc continues its near-flat drift — but the listening does. By three minutes you're not tracking it the way you track most music, waiting for the next event. The hold becomes total. The piece is doing something most music avoids: giving you nothing to anticipate. No hook approaching, no arrival telegraphed by key change, no architecture orienting you to where you are in the arc. Just the sustained mass of it, identical minute to minute. It works because the harmonic field is warm enough that there's nowhere you want to escape to.

By four minutes the question of when something will happen has dissolved. You are inside it. The piece holds the way a sustained tone holds the air in a room — not by doing anything new, but by continuing to be there with enough mass that leaving would require effort. Six minutes and forty-one seconds between the opening silence and the first pattern break. Just hold.

Then at 6:47: five pattern breaks in eight seconds.

6:47, 6:49, 6:51, 6:53, 6:54. The intervals contracting from two seconds to one. Each break registers as almost entirely rhythmic — beat:1.00 across all five, barely any spectral or energy shift. The harmonic field doesn't change. The warmth stays. What stops is not the sound but the underlying structure that has been organizing time — the beat grid releasing, cycling completing, the clock stopping while the room is still warm.

By 6:54 the last break has passed. The piece still sounds like itself. But it has stopped organizing itself.

At 6:55: 4.37 seconds of silence. -80.0dB — the floor, the deepest quiet in the catalog. The piece running 4.37 seconds past the last beat cycle, the sound continuing into the space where the rhythm used to be, and then that too is done.
